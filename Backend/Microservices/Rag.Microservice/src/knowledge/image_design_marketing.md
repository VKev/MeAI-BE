# Image Design — Marketing Style

> Best-practice heuristics. Apply for promotional / sales / launch / CTA-driven posts where the image must do the selling on its own (caption is secondary, viewer might not read it).
> Last reviewed: 2026-05-03

The `marketing` style is the sales flyer. The image must communicate the offer, the brand, the value proposition, and the next action — even if the viewer never expands the caption. Think of it as a poster, a digital ad, or a direct-mail flyer.

---

## When To Use Marketing Style

**Meaning:** Pick this style when the post has a specific commercial goal and the image must close the loop on its own.

### Best fit
- Product launches, new arrivals
- Sales / discounts / limited-time offers
- Service offerings ("Book a free consultation")
- Event announcements (date + venue + RSVP link)
- Lead magnets ("Download the free guide")
- B2B service ads
- Direct-response posts (ROI is measured in clicks/replies)

### Wrong fit
- Mood / lifestyle posts → use `creative`
- Educational / engagement posts → use `branded`
- Storytelling without a CTA → use `branded`

---

## Marketing Composition Rules

**Meaning:** Layout for fast comprehension. The viewer decides "is this for me?" in under one second.

### Apply
- **F-pattern reading order.** Top-left → top-right → middle → bottom-right (CTA goes bottom-right)
- **Visual hierarchy in 4 layers:**
  1. **Headline** (largest, 3–6 words, the value prop)
  2. **Hero visual** (product / outcome / before-after)
  3. **Supporting copy** (subhead, 1–2 short lines, the proof or detail)
  4. **CTA + brand block** (action button + logo + contact line)
- **Aspect ratio:** 1:1 for feed (universal), 4:5 for IG portrait, 9:16 for reel/story (vertical CTAs), 16:9 for FB cover / LinkedIn ad
- **Mobile-first.** Headline must be readable at 200px wide
- **Safe area:** keep bottom 10% (FB/IG UI overlays) and top 10% (story UI) of the frame clear of critical text

---

## Marketing Text Rules

**Meaning:** This is the only style where the image carries multiple text layers. Hierarchy must be obvious at a glance.

### Apply

**Headline (mandatory)**
- 3–6 words maximum
- The value proposition or the offer ("50% off all lenses", "Book your shoot today")
- 80–120pt on a 1080×1080 canvas
- Brand display font, weight: bold

**Subhead (optional)**
- 4–10 words
- The proof, the detail, the qualifier ("Free shipping over $50", "Only 12 spots left")
- 28–36pt
- Sans-serif, regular weight

**CTA button (mandatory)**
- 1–3 words ("Shop Now", "Book Today", "Get Started", "Order Online")
- Drawn as an actual button shape — rounded rectangle, brand-color fill, white text
- Bottom-right or bottom-center
- 32–40pt text inside

**Contact line (mandatory)**
- The page's website + at least one of phone / email
- Format: `meai.website  ·  hello@meai.com  ·  +84 ...` (use middle-dot or pipe separator)
- 16–20pt, sits at the very bottom OR right under the CTA
- Lower contrast OK (gray-on-white is fine — this is reference info, not the lead)

**Total text limit:** Roughly 25 words across headline + subhead + CTA + contact. Beyond that, viewers stop reading.

---

## Marketing Brand Presence

**Meaning:** Brand must be unmistakable. Logo + name + colors + tagline if present.

### Apply
- **Brand logo lockup** — full logo with wordmark, top-left or paired with the CTA. 12–18% of frame width
- **Brand colors enforced** — primary brand color must appear in the CTA button AND somewhere else (a stripe, the headline color, an underline). Repetition signals intentionality
- **Tagline allowed** — a 3–6 word brand tagline can sit under the logo (small)
- **Consistent across the marketing campaign** — if running a series, the logo placement and color block stay in the same spot across all images

---

## Marketing Visual Anchors

**Meaning:** The hero visual must support the offer, not distract from it.

### Apply
- **Product shot:** clean background, single product, well-lit, NOT a busy lifestyle scene
- **Before/after:** split-frame, clear labels above each side
- **Outcome shot:** the customer holding/using the product, smiling, eye contact with camera (high-trust signal)
- **Discount badge / sticker:** for sales — circular badge top-right with "-50%" or "FREE" — high contrast, brand color
- **Avoid:** stock photography that does not feature the actual product/service

---

## Marketing Color & Contrast

**Meaning:** High contrast wins clicks. Subtlety loses to noise in the feed.

### Apply
- **High-contrast text-to-background** — minimum WCAG AAA (7:1) for headlines
- **CTA button must POP** — pick the color with highest contrast against the background, NOT the most "on brand" pastel
- **Brand color discipline** — 2–3 colors max, but allow one accent color that does not appear in the brand kit if it boosts CTA contrast
- **Avoid pastels for headlines** — soft palettes belong in `creative` and `branded`, not `marketing`

---

## Marketing Trust Signals (Optional Add-Ons)

**Meaning:** Marketing posts win on credibility. Sprinkle one or two signals if they fit.

### Apply (pick at most 2)
- **Social proof:** "★★★★★ 4.9/5 from 2,400 reviews"
- **Scarcity:** "Only 12 left" / "Ends Sunday"
- **Guarantee:** "30-day money back" / "Free returns"
- **Authority:** "As featured in [publication]" / "Used by 10,000+ creators"
- Place above or below the CTA, smaller than the CTA itself
- Do not stack more than 2 — too many trust signals reads as desperate
