# Platform Algorithm Signals

> What each platform's ranker actually optimizes for. Use this to decide what to design for: comments, saves, watch time, replies, etc. These signals shift quarterly — verify against the platform's own creator-tools tab if a campaign is critical.
> Last reviewed: 2026-05-03

## Facebook

**Primary signals:** Meaningful interactions > watch time > engagement velocity > recency

### What the ranker rewards

- **Meaningful interactions:** Comments and replies count more than likes. Long comments count more than short ones. Replying to your own comments amplifies further.
- **Native uploads beat external links:** Videos uploaded directly to FB get 5–10x the reach of YouTube link reposts. Same for image uploads vs link previews.
- **FB Reels:** Watch-completion rate, replays, and shares dominate. The first 3 seconds determine whether the reel enters wider distribution.
- **Page posts vs personal posts:** Page posts have lower organic reach (~5%) by default. Boosting reach requires either ad spend or strong meaningful-interaction signals.
- **Group posts:** Often outperform page posts in organic reach if the group is active.

### What gets penalized

- Engagement bait ("comment YES if you agree") — Facebook's classifier flags these and suppresses
- Clickbait headlines that don't match the link content
- Re-posts of viral content from other accounts (low original-content score)
- Links to news / political content for non-news pages

### Tactical takeaways

- End every post with a specific, non-bait question
- Upload videos natively, not as YouTube embeds
- Reply to every comment in the first hour
- For Reels: hook in first 1.5 seconds, target 90%+ completion on short (<15s) clips

## Instagram

**Primary signals:** Saves > Shares > Comments > Likes; for Reels: watch-loops + completion

### What the ranker rewards

- **Saves:** The strongest signal in the feed algorithm. Carousels are heavily favored because they generate more saves per impression than single-image posts.
- **Shares (DMs and Stories):** Second-strongest signal. Posts shared via DM signal "this is so good my friend needs to see it."
- **Reels:** Watch-loop rate (people watching a 7-second reel 3x = 21 seconds of watch time = strong signal). Completion rate matters but loops weight higher.
- **Stories:** Reply-rate is the dominant signal. Stickers (polls, quizzes, sliders) boost engagement floor and feed-back into reach.
- **Account-level signals:** The ranker considers your past 90-day post performance. New posts inherit some baseline from your account's quality score.

### What gets penalized

- "Reposted from [other account]" content — flagged as low-originality
- Posts with very low engagement in the first hour (<5% engagement rate by view)
- Posts that get reported as misleading or spam
- Excessive hashtags (the cap is 30, but using 15+ now correlates with reduced reach)

### Tactical takeaways

- Carousels for educational content (saves)
- Reels for reach (watch-loops)
- Stories for community (reply-rate)
- 5–10 hashtags, not 30
- Post during peak audience time (check Insights for your specific audience)

## TikTok

**Primary signals:** Completion rate > rewatches > shares > comments > likes; first 3-second retention is critical

### What the ranker rewards

- **Completion rate:** Percentage of viewers who watched to the end. The single most important signal.
- **Rewatches:** Loops on short videos. A 7-second video watched 3 full times = 21 seconds of total watch.
- **Shares (in-app and to other platforms):** Strong amplification signal.
- **Comments:** Especially comment threads where users reply to each other.
- **Velocity in first hour:** TikTok's For You Page uses early engagement velocity to decide whether to escalate distribution. A video that gets 1000 views and 100 likes in the first hour will get pushed to 100k+ views.

### What gets penalized

- Watermarks from other platforms (Instagram, YouTube logos) — TikTok suppresses cross-posted content
- Very long videos (>3 min) without high completion — better to keep under 60s if completion is the goal
- Banned audio / music (TikTok actively removes copyrighted audio)
- "Soft" content (slow openings, no payoff) — first-3-second retention drops, reach dies

### Tactical takeaways

- Hook in first 1 second — visual or audio shock
- Keep videos 7–15 seconds for maximum completion + rewatch loops
- Use trending audio (boosts initial reach)
- Strip Instagram watermarks before posting
- Reply to top comments in-app (creates new comment-reply videos that surface the original)

## Threads

**Primary signals:** Reply-rate > quote-replies > likes; trending-topic association

### What the ranker rewards

- **Reply-rate:** How many viewers reply to the post (vs just liking). The dominant signal — Threads is conversation-first.
- **Quote-replies:** When users quote-reply, they amplify your post into their followers' feeds. Highest-leverage engagement type.
- **Reply-thread depth:** Replies that themselves get replies generate more total impressions.
- **Trending-topic association:** Posts that mention currently-trending topics get a temporary boost.
- **Account follow growth:** New follows from a single post is a signal of quality content.

### What gets penalized

- Pure self-promotion without value (link-spam style posts)
- Reposts of Twitter/X content (Threads suppresses cross-posted content for originality)
- Long monologues without reply hooks (5-paragraph essays underperform 1-paragraph hot takes with a question)
- Negative engagement (mass mutes, blocks) — strong negative signal

### Tactical takeaways

- Open with an opinion, end with a hook for replies
- Quote-reply other accounts to participate in trending conversations
- Keep posts short (1–3 lines) for highest reply-rate
- Mention trending topics when relevant to your niche
- Don't cross-post X content verbatim — adapt the framing

## YouTube (for future expansion)

**Primary signals:** Click-through rate (CTR) on thumbnail + average view duration; first-30-second retention

### What the ranker rewards

- **CTR:** Thumbnail + title combination must earn the click
- **Average view duration:** Watch-minutes is the currency of YouTube
- **First-30-second retention:** Drop-off curves from analytics directly affect whether a video is recommended further
- **Session time:** Videos that lead viewers to watch ANOTHER YouTube video after (not leave the platform) get extra boost
- **Subscribers gained per video:** Strong long-term ranking signal

### What gets penalized

- Misleading thumbnails / titles (clickbait that doesn't pay off causes high drop-off)
- Reused TikTok / Reels content reformatted as Shorts without optimization
- Duplicate audio/video across uploads

### Tactical takeaways

- Spend disproportionate effort on the thumbnail (test multiple variants)
- Hook in the first 15 seconds — verbalize what the viewer will get
- Pattern-interrupt every 30 seconds in long videos
- End with "next video" recommendation that matches the same audience
