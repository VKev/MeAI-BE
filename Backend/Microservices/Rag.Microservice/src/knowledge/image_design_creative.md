# Image Design — Creative Style

> Best-practice heuristics. Apply for posts that prioritize visual storytelling, mood, brand vibe, and emotional resonance over information delivery.
> Last reviewed: 2026-05-03

The `creative` style is for editorial / lifestyle / behind-the-scenes / artistic posts. The image carries the message visually; copy lives in the caption, not on the image. Think of it as a magazine spread page, not a poster.

---

## When To Use Creative Style

**Meaning:** Pick this style when the goal is brand vibe, emotional connection, or mood — not conversion or education.

### Best fit
- Behind-the-scenes glimpses, team moments, brand storytelling
- Lifestyle / aspirational imagery
- Brand-vibe posts (no specific product CTA)
- Photography-led brands (food, fashion, travel, fitness)
- Trending-format participation (memes, cultural moments)

### Wrong fit
- Product launches with feature claims → use `marketing`
- Tip / list posts → use `branded`
- Sales / discount announcements → use `marketing`

---

## Creative Composition Rules

**Meaning:** Treat the image like a single editorial photograph. One subject, strong composition, atmosphere over information.

### Apply
- **Single hero subject** — one clear focal point, not a crowded layout
- **Rule of thirds** — subject sits on a one-third intersection, not centered
- **Negative space** — let the subject breathe; do not fill every pixel
- **Cinematic framing** — shallow depth of field, leading lines, layered foreground/background
- **Natural lighting** — golden hour, window light, soft diffused; avoid flat overhead light
- **Aspect ratio** — 1:1 for feed, 4:5 for IG portrait, 9:16 for reels/stories. Marketing-style 16:9 is rarely correct here

---

## Creative Text Rules

**Meaning:** The image should be readable WITHOUT text. Any text on the image is a constraint, not a feature.

### Apply
- **No text by default.** The caption carries copy
- **Allowed exceptions:** A single short word/phrase as artistic typography (e.g., a title like "Sunday." or "On the road"), under 5 words, treated as a design element not a marketing claim
- **No headline + body + CTA stacks** — that is `marketing` territory
- **No contact info on the image** — phone, email, website never appear here. They live in the caption (and only when the post warrants it)
- **No logo lockup with tagline** — at most a small subtle wordmark in a corner; never the full brand block

---

## Creative Color & Mood

**Meaning:** Palette is dictated by the brand identity from the page profile, not invented per post.

### Apply
- **Pull palette from past posts** — use the RAG'd reference images to lock palette
- **2–3 colors max** — limited palette feels intentional
- **Mood signals:**
  - Warm tones (golden, amber) → cozy, nostalgic, food, lifestyle
  - Cool tones (blue, teal, slate) → tech, premium, trustworthy
  - High saturation → energetic, youthful, bold
  - Desaturated / muted → editorial, timeless, premium
- **Match the brand mood from the profile.** A wellness brand should not get a high-contrast neon image; a tech brand should not get a watercolor pastel image

---

## Creative Brand Presence

**Meaning:** Brand is felt, not displayed. The viewer should know it is your brand from the visual style alone.

### Apply
- **No on-image logo** by default. If the brand logo is included, it is small (under 8% of frame), in a corner, low-contrast against the background
- **Brand colors visible** in palette, not as labels
- **Recognizable signature elements** if the brand has them (a recurring prop, a specific filter, a typeface used as artistic typography only)
- **No frames, badges, or banners** — those are `marketing` patterns
