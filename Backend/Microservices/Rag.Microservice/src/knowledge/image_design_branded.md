# Image Design — Branded Style (DEFAULT)

> Best-practice heuristics. This is the DEFAULT style for posts where no other style is explicitly requested. Apply when the post is a regular content post (engagement, light education, news, story) — not pure mood and not a sales push.
> Last reviewed: 2026-05-03

The `branded` style is the safe, all-purpose middle ground. The image must look polished and be visually compelling on its own (like `creative`), but ALSO carries a small brand mark and may include one short headline so a scrolling viewer immediately knows the topic. Think of it as a magazine cover, not a magazine spread (creative) and not a flyer (marketing).

---

## When To Use Branded Style

**Meaning:** Default for everyday posts. Pick this when neither pure-vibe (creative) nor sales-flyer (marketing) is right.

### Best fit
- Tip / advice posts ("3 things you should know about X")
- News / updates / announcements (non-promotional)
- Storytelling posts with a hook
- Industry insights, opinion takes
- Engagement-bait questions paired with a visual
- Anything when the user has not specified a style

### Wrong fit
- Behind-the-scenes mood pieces with no message → use `creative`
- Discount / launch / limited-time offer → use `marketing`

---

## Branded Composition Rules

**Meaning:** Strong photographic / illustrative composition first, brand layer second.

### Apply
- **One hero subject** plus space for one short headline
- **Headline-safe area:** Top third or bottom third of the frame should be visually quiet (sky, surface, gradient, blurred background) so headline text sits readably
- **Rule of thirds** for the subject; do not center
- **Aspect ratio:** 1:1 for feed (default), 4:5 for IG portrait, 9:16 for reel/story covers, 16:9 for FB cover
- **Mobile-first.** Subject must be recognizable at 200px wide

---

## Branded Text Rules

**Meaning:** One short headline, optionally one short subhead. Never a full text block, never CTAs, never contact info.

### Apply
- **One headline** of 3–8 words. Examples: *"Your camera, smarter."* / *"Trick #2: light from the side."* / *"5 things every creator misses."*
- **Optional subhead** of 4–10 words for context, sitting smaller below the headline
- **No body copy.** No paragraphs. No bullet lists
- **No CTA buttons.** ("Shop Now", "Learn More" → that is `marketing`)
- **No phone / email / website on the image.** Contact info lives only in the caption
- **Type hierarchy:**
  - Headline: 60–80pt (on a 1080×1080 canvas)
  - Subhead: 24–32pt
  - Both top-aligned or bottom-aligned, never floating mid-image
- **Font:** Brand display font for headline; clean sans-serif for subhead. Only ONE font family per image (use weight + size for hierarchy)
- **Contrast:** Text must score WCAG AA against its background — dark text on light, light text on dark, never gray on gray

---

## Branded Brand Presence

**Meaning:** Brand mark is visible but does not dominate.

### Apply
- **Wordmark or logo** in one corner (top-left or bottom-right preferred), 6–10% of frame width
- **Brand color accent** somewhere in the frame — a stripe, a button shape, an underline under the headline — to repeat brand identity
- **No tagline lockup** — just the mark
- **No "Follow @handle"** banner
- **Brand consistency check:** the image should be recognizable as YOUR brand from a 500ms glance, before any text is read

---

## Branded Color & Lighting

**Meaning:** Pull palette + lighting from the brand identity (RAG'd from past posts). Branded posts should look like they belong in the same grid as everything else.

### Apply
- **Palette:** Pull primary color from page profile / past-post reference images. Use 2–3 colors max
- **Lighting:** Match the brand's typical mood. Warm + natural for lifestyle brands, cool + clean for tech, high-contrast for bold/edgy brands
- **Headline color:** brand primary OR pure white/black for max readability over photography
- **Background:** photo, illustration, or solid brand color — not a stock gradient

---

## Branded Layout Patterns

**Meaning:** Three reliable patterns work for ~80% of branded posts. Use one, do not invent.

### Apply

**Pattern A — Top-Title Photo**
- Top third: brand color or white block + headline
- Bottom two-thirds: hero photo
- Brand mark: bottom-right corner
- Best for: tip posts, news

**Pattern B — Bottom-Title Photo**
- Top two-thirds: hero photo
- Bottom third: brand color block + headline + subhead
- Brand mark: top-left or in the bottom block
- Best for: storytelling, lifestyle-with-message

**Pattern C — Overlay-Title Photo**
- Full-bleed photo with a subtle dark gradient at top OR bottom
- Headline sits over the gradient area, full-bleed safe
- Brand mark: opposite corner from headline
- Best for: editorial-feel posts, when the photo is too good to crop
