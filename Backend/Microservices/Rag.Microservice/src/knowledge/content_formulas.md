# Content Formula Knowledge Base (English)

> Best-practice copywriting heuristics. Use as a writing structure when relevant; pick whichever fits the question and the platform best.
> Last reviewed: 2026-05-03

## 1. FAB Formula

**Meaning:** Highlight product/service value through features, advantages, and benefits.

### Structure

- **F – Features:** What the product/service has.
- **A – Advantages:** What makes it better than competitors.
- **B – Benefits:** What positive outcomes customers receive.

### Best Use Cases

- Product descriptions
- Sales pages
- E-commerce posts
- Product launch campaigns
- Instagram product captions
- Facebook conversion posts

### Core Psychology

Customers care more about benefits than features.

### Example

- Feature: Waterproof smartwatch
- Advantage: Lasts 10 days on one charge
- Benefit: No need to charge daily while traveling

---

## 2. FAS Formula

**Meaning:** Convert pain points into purchase intent by showing a solution.

### Structure

- **F / P – Problem:** Identify customer pain point
- **A – Agitate:** Intensify the pain/problem
- **S – Solve:** Present the solution

### Best Use Cases

- Ads
- TikTok hooks
- Facebook sales posts
- Landing pages
- Service marketing

### Core Psychology

People act faster to remove pain than to gain pleasure.

### Example

- Problem: Low engagement on social media
- Agitate: Your brand looks invisible online
- Solve: Use our content growth system

---

## 3. BAB Formula

**Meaning:** Show transformation from before to after.

### Structure

- **B – Before:** Current painful state
- **A – After:** Desired future state
- **B – Bridge:** Your product/service bridges the gap

### Best Use Cases

- Beauty
- Fitness
- Coaching
- SaaS case studies
- Reels/TikTok transformation content

### Core Psychology

People buy results and progress.

### Example

Before: Messy finances
After: Full control of money
Bridge: Budget tracking app

---

## 4. 4C Formula

**Meaning:** High-quality writing standards.

### Structure

- **Clear:** Easy to understand
- **Concise:** Short and direct
- **Compelling:** Persuasive and engaging
- **Credible:** Trustworthy with proof

### Best Use Cases

- All content writing
- Brand communication
- Captions
- Threads posts
- Website copy

### Core Psychology

Confused people do not buy.

---

## 5. 4U Formula

**Meaning:** Create irresistible headlines/hooks.

### Structure

- **Useful:** Valuable to audience
- **Urgent:** Time-sensitive or immediate need
- **Unique:** Different from others
- **Ultra-specific:** Specific and concrete

### Best Use Cases

- TikTok hooks
- Reels titles
- Ad headlines
- Email subjects
- Threads openers

### Core Psychology

Specific promises outperform vague claims.

### Example

3 ways to grow followers in 7 days

---

## 6. A FOREST Formula

**Meaning:** Persuasive writing framework using rhetorical devices.

### Structure

- **A – Alliteration:** Repeated sounds
- **F – Facts:** Truth/data
- **O – Opinions:** Arguments/viewpoints
- **R – Repetition:** Repeat key ideas
- **E – Examples:** Examples/case studies
- **S – Statistics:** Numbers/data
- **T – Threes:** Use groups of three

### Best Use Cases

- Long-form sales pages
- Landing pages
- Speeches
- Persuasive copy
- Educational content

### Core Psychology

Repetition + proof + rhythm improve persuasion.

---

## 7. AIDA Formula

**Meaning:** Classic conversion funnel copywriting model.

### Structure

- **A – Attention:** Get noticed
- **I – Interest:** Build curiosity
- **D – Desire:** Create want/need
- **A – Action:** Ask for action

### Best Use Cases

- Ads
- Sales pages
- Product pages
- Reels scripts
- CTAs

### Core Psychology

People buy after emotional desire + clear next step.

---

## 8. 5 Objections Formula

**Meaning:** Remove common buying resistance.

### Structure

- I don't have money
- I don't have time
- I don't like it
- I don't trust it
- I don't need it

### Best Use Cases

- Sales copy
- Objection handling posts
- FAQ content
- Landing pages
- Facebook conversion posts

### Core Psychology

Sales happen when resistance is lowered.

---

## 9. 3 Reasons Why Formula

**Meaning:** Build logic and trust through 3 answers.

### Structure

- Why is your product the best?
- Why should I buy it?
- Why should I trust you?

### Best Use Cases

- Comparison ads
- Short-form video
- Product pages
- Carousel posts
- Threads arguments

### Core Psychology

Buyers need confidence, value, trust.

---

## 10. 4P Formula

**Meaning:** Strong sales persuasion formula.

### Structure

- **Picture:** Show attractive vision/result
- **Promise:** Make clear promise
- **Prove:** Provide evidence
- **Push:** CTA / urge action

### Best Use Cases

- Facebook ads
- Sales posts
- Landing pages
- Product launches

### Core Psychology

Vision + proof + urgency convert well.

---

## 11. 3S Formula

**Meaning:** Story-based persuasion.

### Structure

- **Star:** Main character
- **Story:** Journey/conflict
- **Solution:** Resolution using your offer

### Best Use Cases

- Storytelling posts
- Founder stories
- Facebook posts
- Reels storytelling
- Case studies

### Core Psychology

Humans remember stories more than claims.

---

## 12. 4A Formula

**Meaning:** Brand action loop model.

### Structure

- **Aware:** Know the brand
- **Attitude:** Build positive feeling
- **Act:** Purchase/action
- **Act Again:** Repeat purchase

### Best Use Cases

- Retention strategy
- Brand campaigns
- CRM content
- Loyalty marketing

### Core Psychology

Repeat buyers are more valuable than first-time buyers.

---

## 13. 5A Formula

**Meaning:** Modern customer journey model (Philip Kotler).

### Structure

- **Awareness:** Know brand
- **Appeal:** Feel attracted
- **Ask:** Research and compare
- **Action:** Buy
- **Advocate:** Recommend brand

### Best Use Cases

- Full funnel campaigns
- Community building
- Brand ecosystems
- Social proof systems

### Core Psychology

Modern buyers research and share publicly.

---

## 14. APP Formula

**Meaning:** Fast hook structure for modern content.

### Structure

- **Agree:** Show you understand their problem
- **Promise:** Promise value/solution
- **Preview:** Explain what they'll get

### Best Use Cases

- TikTok intros
- Reels hooks
- Blog intros
- Threads opening posts
- Email intros

### Core Psychology

People engage when they feel understood first.

### Example

Struggling to grow on Instagram?
I'll show you a method that works.
Here are 3 steps to start today.

- AIDA
- BAB
- APP
- FAB

When the goal is to grab attention in the first 1–3 seconds:

- APP
- 3 Reasons Why

When the goal is to build trust through narrative or vulnerability:

## Category Low Priority Short Social

Avoid these in short-form social content (too long-form / too strategic):

- A FOREST
- 4A
- 5A (except brand strategy or Instagram post)
